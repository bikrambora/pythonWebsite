Hypothesis: Prostate cancer is the most common type of cancer

Proved True: Prostate cancer is the most common type of cancer Prostate cancer effects the most people in spite of only effecting males 12% of all cancers. Bowel, lung and breast cancer also occur significantly more commonly than other cancers.

Hypothesis: Prostate cancer has the highest mortality Rate

Proved False: Prostate cancer has a relatively low mortality rate. Lung cancer is not as proliferant it is much more deadly.

Hypothesis: Prostate cancer on the increase

Proved False: Prostate cancer has increased year on year since 2000 but in 2010 cases had decreased by 1,901 compared to 2009.

Hypothesis: Lung Cancer still on the rise

Proved True: Unforunately lung cancer continues to increase year on year despite the introduction of plain packaging on cigarette boxes. The good news is that the rate of increase per year is decreasing.

Hypothesis: Males at higher risk of contracting cancer

Proved True: Just over 68% of all cancer diagnosises are in men while only 32% of cancers occur in women
